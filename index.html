Who We Are

Our Mission

NPO UJA — United Japanese Researchers Around the World ↗

SSH operates through UJA, a nonprofit network of over 6,000 members composed primarily of researchers, with additional members from healthcare, industry, and government sectors, spanning North America, Europe, Asia, and Oceania.

Japan maintains world-class scientific and medical research capabilities, yet faces persistent structural challenges in translating discoveries into societal and economic impact — including limited cross-sector mobility, insufficient integration into global innovation networks, and a shortage of professionals with hybrid expertise spanning science, business, and policy.

SSH creates a neutral platform for dialogue, knowledge exchange, and partnership formation across borders and sectors. By mobilizing globally distributed Japanese talent, we aim to contribute to Japan's long-term innovation capacity, global competitiveness, and leadership in emerging scientific domains.

The Opportunity

Four Frontiers SSH Is Opening

Japan's scientific talent is exceptional. SSH exists to unlock its full potential by expanding four interconnected frontiers — each one a concrete area where human connection and dialogue can create lasting change.

①

Expanding the Translation Mindset

Japan's researchers and clinicians hold enormous untapped potential to drive innovation. SSH opens the door to new perspectives — entrepreneurship, policy influence, and diverse pathways for scientific ideas to reach society.

②

Growing Bridge-Builder Talent

Professionals who move fluently between science, medicine, business, and policy are the architects of a better ecosystem. SSH creates the conversations and connections where that fluency develops.

③

Connecting Japan's Global Community

Japan's scientific community has the potential to become one of the key hubs within global innovation networks. SSH currently focuses on connecting Japanese researchers and professionals distributed worldwide — building the trust and relationships that will, over time, grow to engage diverse global stakeholders.

④

Fostering Cross-Disciplinary Encounters

SSH brings together researchers from across all disciplines — natural sciences, humanities, social sciences, and beyond — to spark new ideas, research seeds, and collaborative ventures through open dialogue.

Founder's Message

Why I Started SSH

Progress in pediatric oncology has not come from oncology research alone.

The advancement of pediatric oncology has been driven not only by cancer research, but by contributions from surgery, radiology, pathology, infectious disease, engineering, and many other fields. This led me to believe that by connecting the ideas of researchers from diverse disciplines to society, those ideas will one day reach pediatric oncology — and that belief is what led me to start this initiative.

Nobuhiro Tanno, MD, PhD
Founder, Science to Society Hub

What Sets Us Apart

Unique Value Proposition

🌏

Global Japanese Network

Backed by UJA's 6,000+ member network spanning major academic and biotech hubs across 4 continents — the largest global Japanese researcher community.

👥

Radically Diverse Participants

Researchers, physicians, hospital clinicians, pharma/biotech professionals, corporate leaders, startup founders, VCs, support organizations, and government officials — all in one room.

🗣️

Bidirectional Dialogue

SSH is a genuine two-way dialogue platform — not a lecture series. Speakers and participants engage in substantive exchange that produces real connections and actionable insights.

🗾

Japan-Based, Globally Connected

Currently conducted primarily in Japanese to maximize depth of discussion. English-language sessions are actively planned as SSH expands to engage non-Japanese stakeholders.

🌐

Multi-Country Focus

Each session focuses on a specific country or region — drawing from the U.S., Europe, Oceania, Asia, and beyond — to share first-hand perspectives on local academic environments and innovation ecosystems.

⚡

Bottom-Up Ecosystem Building

SSH focuses on human capital development and international connectivity as the foundation for bottom-up ecosystem building.
