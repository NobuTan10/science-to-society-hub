Who We Are

Our Mission

NPO UJA — United Japanese Researchers Around the World ↗

SSH operates through UJA, a nonprofit network of over 6,000 members composed primarily of researchers, with additional members from healthcare, industry, and government sectors, spanning North America, Europe, Asia, and Oceania.

Japan maintains world-class scientific and medical research capabilities, yet faces persistent structural challenges in translating discoveries into societal and economic impact — including limited cross-sector mobility, insufficient integration into global innovation networks, and a shortage of professionals with hybrid expertise spanning science, business, and policy.

SSH creates a neutral platform for dialogue, knowledge exchange, and partnership formation across borders and sectors. By mobilizing globally distributed Japanese talent, we aim to contribute to Japan's long-term innovation capacity, global competitiveness, and leadership in emerging scientific domains.

Three Program Pillars

01

Foster Japan's Life Science Ecosystem

Introduce researchers and clinicians to innovation, entrepreneurship, and translation pathways. Connect them with VCs, startup founders, and tech-transfer experts.

02

Connect to Global Ecosystems

Create opportunities for Japanese researchers and startups to interact with VCs, universities, and accelerators in North America, Europe, Singapore, and beyond.

03

Promote Interdisciplinary Innovation

Bridge life sciences with engineering, data science, materials science, and public policy. Generate new research seeds and startup concepts through cross-disciplinary dialogue.

Why SSH Exists

Japan's Ecosystem Challenges

Japan has world-class science — yet structural barriers prevent discoveries from reaching society. SSH was founded to directly address these six challenges.

①

Low Awareness of Translation

Researchers and clinicians have limited interest in commercialization and societal application of their work.

②

Lack of Hybrid Talent

Shortage of professionals with cross-disciplinary experience spanning science, business, and policy.

③

Weak Global Connectivity

Insufficient ties to overseas VCs, startups, and accelerators leave Japanese science isolated from global ecosystems.

④

Limited Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Innovation from cross-disciplinary collaboration with engineering, data science, and other fields remains underdeveloped.

⑤

Funding Scale & Speed Gap

R&D investment in Japan lags significantly behind the U.S. and other global competitors in both scale and speed.

⑥

Underdeveloped Tech Transfer

University and research institution support structures for commercialization remain immature compared to global peers.

SSH directly addresses these barriers — focusing on human capital development and international connectivity, two levers capable of creating impact faster than top-down policy reform.

What Sets Us Apart

Unique Value Proposition

🌏

Global Japanese Network

Backed by UJA's 6,000+ member network spanning major academic and biotech hubs across 4 continents — the largest global Japanese researcher community.

👥

Radically Diverse Participants

Researchers, physicians, hospital clinicians, pharma/biotech professionals, corporate leaders, startup founders, VCs, support organizations, and government officials — all in one room.

🗣️

Bidirectional Dialogue

SSH is a genuine two-way dialogue platform — not a lecture series. Speakers and participants engage in substantive exchange that produces real connections and actionable insights.

🗾

Japan-Based, Globally Connected

Currently conducted primarily in Japanese to maximize depth of discussion. English-language sessions are actively planned as SSH expands to engage non-Japanese stakeholders.

🌐

Multi-Country Focus

Each session spotlights a specific country or region — U.S., Singapore, Europe, and beyond — enabling comparative learning and direct partnership formation with each ecosystem.

⚡

Bottom-Up Ecosystem Building

Where top-down policy changes are slow, SSH focuses on human capital development and international connectivity — domains that create impact quickly and durably.
